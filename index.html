About Me

Risk brings Rizk — a phrase that's not just a clever play on my name, but the story of my life.

The word "Rizk" (رزق) in Arabic means livelihood — the sustenance and opportunities that come our way, often through perseverance, patience, and sometimes... risk. For me, it's a daily reminder that embracing uncertainty can lead to purpose and transformation — even after major setbacks.

After nearly a decade working as a journalist, my career came to an abrupt halt due to uncontrollable circumstances. I lost my job — but refused to lose momentum. Instead, I chose to rebuild — from scratch.

I pivoted into tech, enrolling in a full-stack web development bootcamp at George Washington University, where I discovered my passion for cloud computing. After completing multiple internships, I earned the AWS Developer Associate certification and went on to pursue a Master's in Cloud Computing at the University of Maryland Global Campus.

To further strengthen my expertise, I completed industry-recognized programs from Udacity and IBM in cloud and data engineering. Today, I'm building hands-on cloud and data projects, mastering tools like Terraform, Redshift, Glue, Spark, and Airflow, and actively seeking my next opportunity in cloud or data engineering.

I currently have 2.5 years of full-time experience, in addition to 1.5 years of internships across cloud computing, data engineering, and web development.

Every line of code I write carries the same determination that fueled my transformation. I don't just build systems — I build second chances.
